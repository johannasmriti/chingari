Chingari Rehabilitation Centre

In their struggle to safeguard their livelihoods, some of the workers realised early on that the Bhopal gas tragedy had not only compromised their ability to earn a decent living but had also robbed them of their health and well-being.

Neither the government nor Union Carbide took responsibility for ensuring that the gas victims were provided adequate employment, or that their ailments would be cured, or that land and water polluted by the chemical wastes from the factory would be rid of their poisons.

The workers took their struggle against the company to all corners of the country and beyond its shores to the world.

Facilities Provided to the Children:

  • Physiotherapy
  • Occupational Therapy
  • Special Education
  • Speech Therapy
  • Mid-day Meal
  • PIck up and drop facilities

Speech Therapy:

Our Speech therapy helps children who suffer from issues like poor or no speech, attention issues, poor eye contact, Chewing problem, Drooling issues, limited tongue flexibility, poor comprehension etc.

  • At present we have 6 Speech Therapists looking after 210 children
  • Drooling has been cured in 76 children
  • 86 children have started paying attention and maintaining eye contact.
  • 47 children have started talking in phrase
  • 34 children can now talk in sentences
  • About 30 children can now tell small stories

Special Education:

Special Education helps in the Social, Personal, Academic, Recreational & Occupational Development of Children with special needs.

  • At present we have 5 Special Educators and 1 Assistant providing Spl Ed to about 240 children.
  • 40 children can now write alphabets in Hindi and English, numbers, their own names
  • 25 children can now add and subtract, write the names of their family members..
  • 15 severely disabled children have started doing simpler activities like working with blocks.
  • 46 children have started going to normal school.
