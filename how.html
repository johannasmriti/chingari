About Us:

Two of the gas tragedy survivors, Champa Devi Shukla and Rashida Bee, were recognized for their activism on behalf of the thousands of survivors of the Union Carbide Gas Disaster, and were presented with the Goldman Environmental Award in 2004.

The prize money received ($125,000) by the two ladies was jointly donated for the welfare of the gas victims. Chingari Trust was thus born in 2005, and has since then been serving the needs of congenitally disabled children born in the families affected by Bhopal Gas Tragedy and the subsequent water contamination.

Chingari, an all-women's trust, represents the spirit of resilience of the Bhopal survivors, notably the women who have refused to cow down and accept defeat.

'Chingari' is, indeed, the 'flame' to ignite and inspire the world to fight against the irresponsible behaviour and criminal acts of the corporate sector.
